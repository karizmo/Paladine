Japan

If you are indeed travelling to Japan, you would actually be surprised of how Westernized Japan has become. A typical breakfast these days now, consists of bread, rice, or eggs, a meat product such as ham, bacon, or sausage, and a wide array of vegetables. Even after reading through blogs and websites, most japanese citizens usually make up an ideal type of breakfasts for themselves.

A traditional Japanese breakfast is somewhat different from other kind of breakfasts around Asia. It can consist of foods that could be conceivably enjoyed during lunch or dinner. Any traveller who visited Japan may have encountered a breakfast that may have a piece of grilled fish, miso soup, onsen tamago, nori, and japanese pickles.

Steamed Rice (Gohan)

In Japan, it is no surprise that you will be presented in most meals a plain steamed rice, either white (hakumai) or brown rice (genmai). This is an essential dish that basically goes with any other meal you eat.

Grilled Fish (Yakizakana)

Yakizakana is a term for Japanese style grilled fish that is often served whole. Although japan is known best for their raw fish menus, Yakizakana is by far a more common dish than sushi in the lives of most people in Japan. If you travel to Japan, you will likely encounter this in regular meals. The fish is either broiled in the oven or cooked in a pan and simply seasoned with salt.

Geisha

In movies, you may have seen the distinctive white face, red lips and decorated hairstyle of the Geishas. During the edo era, rich merchants of the cities developed the arts of the country and played major roles in urbanizing Japan. From the early stages, these rich merchants developed and trained Geishas into providing service to entertain and charm.

Fermented Soybeans (Natto)

Natto is served over steamed rice and this dish is considered a high protein Japanese breakfast staple. Natto consists of many benefits for your body such as reduce bone loss, reduce blood clots, enhance liver function and encourage the flow of urine. For those ladies who love travelling to Japan, Natto is also known to contain Vitamin PQQ, which is very important for the skin.

Miso Soup (Miso Shiru)

Probably the most iconic type of soup in Japan. The Miso soup consists of tofu, miso paste, and chopped green onion. Miso paste is made from fermented soybeans, sea salt and koji( a mold starter), and often mixed with rice. Some of the benefits of Miso soup is that it contains all essential amino acids, stimulates the secretion of digestive fluids within the stomach, and restores probiotics to the intestines. The list goes on!

Slurp away

Funny how when you slurp your food in Western cultures can be deemed as rude and impolite. Soups and noodles to many are meant to be eaten in a very polite matter which can make it a nuisance. However, in Japan when you slurp on noodles and soup, it is seen as complementary to the chef, so slurp away if you find yourself enjoying the noodles in Japan.

Gion Matsuri

In July, Kyoto is invaded by the Gion Festival, one of the most famous festivals in all of Japan. If you are travelling during this time, you will find yourself surrounded massive amount of parades. Gion Matsuri dates back all the way to 869, when the emperor dedicated 66 pikes and ordered prayers and festivities be carried out at Yasaka Shrine in order to appease the gods to prevent plague. The Festival has been held annually since 970, and with floats from 999.
