Traditional breakfast in the Philippines

Rice which is almost in every breakfast meal is the one thing that ties any Filipino breakfast together. Filipino breakfasts can be sweet or savory and also hearty and filling.

Pan De Sal

Pan del sal which is a sweet bread, is a favourite among filipinos for eating in the morning. It is usually cut and filled with either fried egg, cheese or peanut butter. While eating pan de sal, filipinos often accompany it with some hot coffee where some even dip it in there!

Tocilog

Pork Tocino, an all time favorite of most Filipinos. A cured meat product native to the Philippines. It is usually made out of pork and is similar to ham and bacon. With the tocino are sides of friend rice and fried egg.

Champorado

Champorado is a thick chocolate porridge made with tablea and glutinous rice. It is prepared with puffy, chewy, and slightly sticky grainds of sweet rice. A sweet breakfast indeed, which is sometimes accompanied with tuyo or dried fish.

Ati-Atihan Festival

Held annually in January, Ati-Atihan is to celebrate Santo Niño in Kalibo, Aklan. Consisting of music and dancing with tribal costume, the whole town celebrates for a couple of weeks. The event is originates from religious vantage point, celebrating Santo Niño which is the infant Jesus.

Archipelago

Archipelago means a group of islands, which accurately describes the Philippines. The Philippines has a total of 7,641 islands.
